Senior Design Project: Video Pipeline for Machine Computer Vision

Link to Project Webpage.

I had the priviledge to work with 3 amazing team mates on the Senior Design Project titled Video Pipeline for Machine Computer Vision!

We created a video pipeline that takes in a video stream and outputs a video stream with pupils of people's eyes highlighted (per our client's requirements). It is deployed on an Ultra96v2 FPGA board and uses a MIPI camera sensor to capture the video stream. The project was a great learning experience and allowed us to work with various technologies and tools.

We learned and used Python, OpenCV, and Vitis AI to create the pipeline.

I specifically worked with embedded systems on the FPGA. This included integrating the I/O and two algorithms on the FPGA dev board.

In the big picture, our team contributed to the development of a subsystem that will help wheelchair-bound individuals have a better quality of life.

Projects

Project 1: CyBot Navigates Mars

I collaborated with a team of 3 other engineering students to program the above CyBot to navigate a "Mars-like" evnironment remotely. We leveraged a Texas Instruments M4 series microcontroller to control the CyBot's motors and sensors. Note that the CyBot had an ultrasonic sensor and an infared sensor attached atop of a servo motor to detect its surroundings withour runnning into obstacles. The CyBot was (almost) able to navigate the environment autonomously and avoid obstacles using a combination of sensors and algorithms.

For this project, I contributed to all parts of it. Significant portions I did are: analog to digital conversion algorithms (ADCs), navigation algorithms, and the motor control algorithms. I learned what embedded systems are and general programming skills. I also grew my skillset to work with a team and communicate effectively.

Project 2: Speaker Crossover

I designed the electrical circuitry to separate frequencies from an input to respective low (woofer), medium (mid-range), and high (tweeter) frequency outputs. This was done using a combination of resistors, capacitors, and inductors. The project was part of an electrical engineering lab course, EE 230. I exercised use of a breadboard, power supplies, and elecrtical filters create the circuit. I also learned how to use an oscilloscope to measure the output signals. I fully designed and implemented this circuitry.

Project 3: Develop ALU From VDHL

In CPRE 381, Computer Architecture, I designed, developed, and tested, and debugged the Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU) for my team's processor. This was developed in VDHL Hardware Design Language, tested using model sim, and deployed on an FPGA. I learned VDHL, Model Sim, and perserverence throught this project.
